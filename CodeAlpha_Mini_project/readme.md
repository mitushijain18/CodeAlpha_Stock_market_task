# Mini Project: Trading Strategy Case Study

## Project Overview

This project is completed as part of the CodeAlpha Stock Market & Trading Basics Internship. The project focuses on understanding and analyzing a trading strategy through a case study approach.

The objective of this project is to explore how traders develop strategies using market data, technical indicators, risk management techniques, and trading rules to make informed decisions.

This case study provides practical exposure to trading concepts, strategy evaluation, and market analysis.

## About Trading Strategy

A trading strategy is a systematic approach used by traders to decide when to buy or sell financial assets. Trading strategies are based on market analysis, price movements, technical indicators, and risk management principles.

A well-designed trading strategy helps traders reduce emotional decision-making and follow a structured approach while participating in financial markets.

## Strategy Description

The selected trading strategy works by analyzing market data and identifying potential buying and selling opportunities.

The strategy uses predefined rules based on technical indicators and market conditions. When the conditions are satisfied, a trade decision is generated.

### Entry Conditions

The conditions used to enter a trade include:

- Identification of market trend
- Confirmation through technical indicators
- Favorable price movement
- Risk-to-reward evaluation

### Exit Conditions

The conditions used to exit a trade include:

- Achievement of target price
- Stop-loss activation
- Change in market trend
- Indicator-based exit signals

## Technical Indicators Used

## Risk Management

Risk management is an important part of trading strategy development.

The following techniques were considered:

- Setting stop-loss limits
- Maintaining proper risk-to-reward ratio
- Avoiding excessive trading
- Diversifying investments
- Managing position size
