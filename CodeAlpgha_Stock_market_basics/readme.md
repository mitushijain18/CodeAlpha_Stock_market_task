# Stock Market Basics Research Report

## Project Overview

This project is completed as part of the CodeAlpha Stock Market & Trading Basics Internship. The task focuses on understanding the fundamentals of the stock market, different types of markets, and the role of regulatory authorities in maintaining a fair and transparent financial system.

The objective of this research report is to develop a basic understanding of stock markets, primary and secondary markets, and the role of the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) in regulating the Indian stock market.

## About Stock Market

The stock market is a financial marketplace where shares of publicly listed companies are bought and sold. It allows companies to raise funds for business growth while providing investors with opportunities to invest and earn returns.

The stock market plays an important role in economic development by promoting investment, capital formation, and efficient allocation of financial resources.

In India, major stock exchanges include:

* National Stock Exchange (NSE)
* Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)

These exchanges provide platforms for investors to trade securities in a regulated environment.

## Primary Market

The primary market is where companies issue new securities to raise capital from investors. Companies launch Initial Public Offerings (IPOs), Follow-on Public Offerings (FPOs), and other offerings to collect funds for expansion and development.

### Features of Primary Market

* New shares are issued for the first time
* Money goes directly to the company
* Helps businesses raise capital
* Supports economic growth

## Secondary Market

The secondary market is where already issued securities are traded among investors. Investors buy and sell shares through stock exchanges such as NSE and BSE.

### Features of Secondary Market

* Existing shares are traded
* Provides liquidity to investors
* Prices are determined by demand and supply
* Trading takes place through stock exchanges

## Role of SEBI in India

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulatory authority responsible for managing and developing the securities market in India.

SEBI was established to protect investors, regulate stock exchanges, and ensure fair trading practices.

### Functions of SEBI

* Protects investor interests
* Regulates stock exchanges
* Prevents insider trading
* Controls fraudulent market activities
* Regulates brokers and financial intermediaries
* Ensures transparency in listed companies
* Promotes development of financial markets
